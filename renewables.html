Sustainable Woodwork & Circular Craftsmanship

TEACH's woodwork initiative focuses on empowering local communities through sustainable furniture making and creative reuse of wood industry byproducts. This program is both an income generator and an environmental solution aligned with circular economy principles.

🪵 Wood Furniture Production

Our trainees learn hands-on skills to produce high-quality furniture using responsibly sourced wood and repurposed materials. This provides them with employable skills and opens pathways for entrepreneurship within the carpentry sector.

♻️ Reuse of Sawdust

  • Sawdust is converted into biochar, enhancing soil health and sequestering carbon.
  • Local farmers use sawdust as bedding material for poultry and pigs, contributing to livestock hygiene and cost reduction.

🎓 Training & Impact

Since 2023, five trainees have successfully graduated from our sustainable woodwork program, with plans to grow this number annually. The program not only imparts technical skills but also encourages innovation in sustainable production and waste utilization.
